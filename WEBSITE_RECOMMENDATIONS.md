# Hikari Sushi Website Recommendations

## Research Summary
Based on analysis of 15+ upscale sushi restaurant websites including Nobu, Masa NYC, Uchi, Sushi Nakazawa, Sushi Den, NAOE, O Ya, Sushi Ginza Onodera, The Omakase Room, Hidden Omakase, Katsuya, SushiSamba, Sushi Roku, Matsuhisa, and Coral Omakase.

---

## High Impact / Easy to Add

### 1. Social Media Links (Facebook + Instagram)
- Every upscale sushi site has these
- Recommended placement: Footer brand column + Location/Contact section
- Use inline SVGs for icons (no new dependencies)

### 2. Private Dining / Events Section
- ~90% of upscale sushi sites have this
- Even a simple section describing party options, large group accommodations, or a "Contact us for private events" inquiry would elevate the site
- Include: event inquiry form or email, capacity info, space photos

### 3. Chef / Team Profile
- ~70% of upscale sites feature this
- Short bio of the head chef with photo, background, philosophy, and training
- Customers at upscale sushi places want to know who's behind the counter
- Examples: Masa NYC dedicates an entire page to Chef Masa; NAOE features "Chef Kevin Cory" in primary nav

### 4. Drinks / Sake Menu
- ~65% of upscale sites highlight their beverage program
- If Hikari has sake, Japanese whisky, cocktails, or a curated wine list, this deserves its own menu section
- Biggest signal of "sit-down experience" vs. "takeout spot"
- The Omakase Room promotes "one of Chicago's largest Japanese whisky and sake programs"

### 5. Desserts Section
- Already on TODO list, photos exist (mochi, tiramisu)
- Quick win to round out the menu

### 6. Email Newsletter Signup
- ~75% of upscale sites have this
- Simple "Join our guest list" field in the footer
- For seasonal announcements, special events, and promotions
- Examples: Masa uses "Join our guest list" for "exclusive invitations and seasonal announcements"

---

## Medium Impact / Moderate Effort

### 7. Press / Awards / Accolades
- ~75% of upscale sites showcase these
- Any local press coverage, food blog features, "Best of Utah" mentions, Yelp awards
- Display as social proof with pull quotes
- NAOE displays quotes from Louis Vuitton City Guide, Forbes, Miami Herald, etc.

### 8. Gift Cards
- ~80% of upscale sites offer these
- Could link to a Square/Toast gift card portal or "Gift cards available in-store" mention
- Prominent placement in nav at Nobu, Uchi, Sushi Roku, and O Ya

### 9. Instagram Feed Embed
- Already on TODO list
- ~40% of upscale sites embed their feed
- Great for showing the restaurant is active and food looks amazing in real life

### 10. Lunch Specials Section
- Already on TODO list
- Many upscale sushi spots differentiate their lunch offering
- Could drive weekday traffic

### 11. Omakase / Chef's Tasting Menu
- If Hikari offers (or could offer) an omakase experience, this is the #1 thing that signals "upscale"
- Dedicated section with course count, price, and reservation info
- This is the centerpiece of sites like NAOE, Sushi Nakazawa, and Masa

### 12. Accessibility Statement
- ~60% of upscale sites have one
- Small footer link to a basic accessibility page
- Good practice and increasingly expected

---

## Lower Priority / Aspirational

### 13. FAQ Page
- Hours, parking info, dress code, allergy policies, large party guidelines
- Reduces phone calls and sets expectations for an upscale experience

### 14. Catering Menu
- If Hikari does catering or sushi platters for events/offices
- Dedicated section could drive new revenue

### 15. Virtual Tour / More Ambiance Photos
- Interior shots of the dining space, sushi bar, and atmosphere
- Helps customers picture themselves there before visiting
- Current gallery only shows food - no interior/ambiance shots

### 16. Careers Page
- ~60% of upscale sites have this
- Even a simple "Join our team" with an email address

### 17. Sustainability / Sourcing Story
- Where the fish comes from, freshness commitments, etc.
- NAOE highlights their family soy sauce brewery; Sushi Den flies fish from Japan
- If Hikari has a sourcing story, it's a differentiator
